  34. Night Order

  1. From the highest pits of Voicetext to the town of LivetextWood Bluff, we've been Livetexting on these lands carefree.... That is until evil came and scorched our lands.

    The Livetexters, while considerably different from one another, gather in their odd ways to combat the heavily synchronized evil.

  2. We are Livetext is an odd script, each character needs the other to coordinate, but their abilities for good and evil equally, trample on each other if not well coordinated. Many good characters have night abilities that grant a lot of information, and requires heavy day coordination to get some extra. Outsiders turn against their fellow Townsfolk, and evils hide behind their silent abilities and incredible setup modifiers.

    GOOD PLAYERS will require to trust heavily on each other to achieve their maximum information output. Many characters benefit from survival and trust, resourcing who lives and dies might be difficult. Other Townsfolk benefit from killing each other and piles of lying, nothing is at it seems..... and getting the right executions can be a herculean task with the sheer amount of information. OUTSIDERS play a key role by threading between good and evil, whilst needing to keep their identity secret until the right moment comes.

    EVIL PLAYERS will need to sneak into town, their abilities are not nearly as loud until everything is pieced together, and must benefit from their silence to creep their misinformation and evil recruiting into town. Their minions are nearly undetectable unless town is organized, and demons may suddenly detonate piles of wrong information and/or instantly win on their own.

  3. Atxmiczz

    Each night, choose a player: they and their neighbors are sober, healthy & get true info until dusk.

    “Did you try the medicine drug? Only stupid people try the medicine drug. You are stupid.”

    Atxmiczz cures the town from poison.

    Examples

    • The Atxmiczz chooses Wolf, later that night Wolf is selected by the Poisoner, Wolf is still sober, healthy and can't be poisoned.

    • The Atxmiczz chooses Wolf, which neighbors CosmoThere. Later that night CosmoThere is selected by the Poisoner, CosmoThere is still sober, healthy and can't be poisoned.

    • The Atxmiczz chooses the Savant, during the day they visit the Storyteller and learn two true statements instead of a true and false.

    • The Atxmiczz chooses a player that is poisoned by the Arlene, that poison is nulified until dusk, once dusk arrives, the poison takes effect again.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Atxmiczz, they point at a player, put the SOBER AND HEALTHY reminder on their choice and their respective neighbors. Put Atxmiczz back to sleep.

    • A player marked SOBER AND HEALTHY is sober and healthy (even if they’re also marked DRUNK or POISONED) and always gets true information (even if an ability would make them drunk or poisoned).

  4. Wren

    Each night, choose a player (not yourself): if you only vote with them tomorrow, learn if their neighbors have different character types from each other.

    Wren has a sharp intuition.

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is the Wren, they choose Wolf during the night. During the day, two nominations happen. Wolf and Gold Bars vote yes for both of them. That night, Gold Bars learns a "no" (Wolf's neighbors are Townsfolk and Outsider).

    • Gold Bars is the Wren and chooses Gambit, during the day. Two nominations happen, Gambit votes yes for both, but Gold Bars votes no. They learn no information that night.

    • Gold Bars picks CosmoThere, next night they learn a "yes", because both CosmoThere's neighbors are Townsfolk.

    • Gold Bars picks CosmoThere, next night they learn a "yes", because both CosmoThere's neighbors are Minions.

    • Gold Bars picks CosmoThere, next night they learn a "no", because of one CosmoThere's neighbors is a Minion, while the other is the Demon.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Wren. They point to a player, mark the pointed player with the INTUITION reminder. Then put the Wren to sleep.

    • "if you only vote with them tomorrow" implies that Wren only receives their information long as they only vote when their choice also votes, and vice-versa. You may remove the INTUITION reminder from the chosen player if Wren happens to not follow their vote.

    • During the night, if Wren followed their Intuition's votes last day. Give them a thumbs-up/thumbs-down signal upon waking them to indicate whether or not their Intuition's neighbors are the same character type (Townsfolk/Outsider/Minion/Demon/Traveler) before asking them to point at another player.

  5. That Amnesiac

    Each night, learn a player the Storyteller believes is the most of a specific trait. Each day, privately guess it: you learn how accurate you are.

    “I . AM . ETERNAL.”

    Amnesiac possesses overwhelming power.

    Examples

    • That Amnesiac learns Wolf on the first night. The secret trait is "Likely to spread false information". On the first day, That Amnesiac visits the Storyteller and guesses "A liar", they learn a "Warm".

    • That Amnesiac learns Strawberry on the first night. The secret trait is "Learned the most information". On the first day, That Amnesiac visits the Storyteller and guesses "Probably unkillable", they learn a "Cold".

    • That Amnesiac learns Gambit on the first night. The secret trait is "Funny". On the first day, That Amnesiac visits the Storyteller and guesses "Funny", they learn a perfect "Bingo".

    • That Amnesiac is poisoned, they currently have the trait "Misguided", they learn a completely unrelated player to their trait. During the day they guess "Misguided by evil" and receive a poisoned "Cold".

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Amnesiac. Point to a player. Put the Amnesiac to sleep.

    • Each day, the Amnesiac makes a guess about the trait in private. Answer “Cold”, “Warm”, “Hot", or “Bingo”.

    • The trait can be anything that can be assigned to a player, but generally should be something simple the Amnesiac can guess.

    • You may change the trait at any time, but generally keeping a single consistent trait renders an easier time for the Amnesiac.

  6. Loen

    Each night, choose a player: if they are 'about to die' tomorrow, learn an evil player the following night. If you're "mad" as Loen, lose this ability.

    “When you leave every night in hunt for evil, that's recklessness. When I want to hit your face with this long staff of wood, that's concern.”

    Loen worries about those in danger.

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is the Loen, they select Wolf at night and nominate them during the day, Wolf gets enough votes to be on the block. Someone else is executed during the day. That night, Gold Bars learns that CosmeThere is evil.

    • Wolf is the Loen, they select a player during the night but are not able to make them get enough votes, that night, Wolf does not learn anything.

    • Gambit is the Loen, they nominate their targeted player during the day, but don't elaborate why, town believes Gambit is the Loen. That night, Loen loses their ability.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake up Loen so they can select a player, mark their choice with the TARGET reminder.

    • If the chosen player was 'about to die' (that is, got enough votes to be put on the block) today, Loen learns an evil player tonight.

    • "Madness" stands for "You are trying to convince the group of something". So, if the Loen player is trying to convince the group that they are a Loen, this means that Loen is not following the madness rules.

    • If you believe they are not following the madness rules, they lose their ability!

    • If Loen is poisoned during the day, their ability still works at night. If Loen is poisoned at night, they learn false info that night but still choose a player normally.

    • Exiles don’t count for this ability.

  7. Echo

    Each night*, guess 3 players characters (not yourself): if only one guess is wrong, a player learns this.

    Echo echoes through Ravenswood.

    Examples

    • The Echo chooses Gold Bars as Clockmaker, CosmoThere as Investigator and Wolf as Baron. Only one guess is incorrect, that night, Gambit learns "Echo".

    • The Echo chooses Gold Bars as Investigator, CosmoThere as Baron and Wolf as Imp. Two guesses are incorrect, nothing happens.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Echo and wait out for them pointing towards 3 players and characters on the character sheet. Put the Echo back to sleep. If only one of their guesses were wrong, mark a player with the LEARNS reminder, they learn this.

    Be REALLY careful with which players learn this character, it snowballs REALLY easily.

  8. Moll

    Each night*, if you nominated & executed a player today, you learn a player of their alignment, even if you died tonight.

    “If no one else will act, I will. If others act, I might. We cannot do nothing, then we're leaving all the control to them.”

    Moll is loud and proud.

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is the Moll, they nominate the Clockmaker and gets them executed, tonight, Moll learns the Investigator, as the Clockmaker and Investigator are of the same alignment.

    How to Run

    • If the Moll nominates and executes a player, mark the Moll's target with the EXECUTED reminder. Tonight, wake Moll, they learn a player of the same alignment as the executee.

    • The executed player does not have to die for the Moll's ability to work.

    • Exiles don’t count for this ability.

  9. Star

    Each day, if you publicly guess which players are Minion(s) and which are Demon(s), good wins.

    “I am here because of you, and you are here because of... wait a minute.”

    Star walks, does a backflip and guesses the entire evil team.

    Examples

    • Gambit is the Star, they publicly guess CosmoThere as the minion and Wolf as their demon, Gold Bars is correct, the game immediately ends and good wins.

    • Gold Bars is the Star, they publicly guess CosmoThere as the minion and Wolf as their demon, Gold Bars is incorrect, the game continues.

    • Wolf is the Star, they privately whisper guessing that CosmoThere is the minion and Wolf is their demon, since it is a whisper and not all players can clearly hear it, nothing happens.

    How to Run

    • Each day, once only, if the Alstarhir declares that they wish to use their ability, prompt them to guess which player is the Demon, and which player(s) are Minions. If incorrect, nothing happens and the game continues. If correct, declare that good wins.

    • The Alstarhir’s guesses need to be public, and they need to be during the day. They don’t have to guess every day.

    • Other players may pretend to be the Alstarhir and make a guess. Like the Juggler or the Gossip, the Storyteller will briefly pretend that player is the Alstarhir.

    • If the Alstarhir guesses the Demon player as the Demon, and the Minion players as Minions, the game ends immediately. The Alstarhir must guess all Demon and Minion players.

    • The Alstarhir doesn’t need to guess specific minion characters, nor specific Demon characters.

    • If there is more than one Demon in play, all Demons must be guessed, including dead Demons.

    • If a player is a Minion and Demon, such as Legion, the Alstarhir must guess this player as a Demon.

    • Once a guess is made, the Alstarhir cannot change their mind later that day and guess again.

    • The Alstarhir needs to guess Minions and Demons, even if they are good, but need not guess which Travellers are evil.

    • If the evil team has changed during the game, the Alstarhir must guess the current evil team, not the starting evil team.

  10. Lamb

    Each day, a player may publicly state their character. Tonight, you learn if they were truthful.

    “Her coat white as snow. Her eyes black as night. Should you not be able to bear the weight of a confessional, you may always turn to the lamb.”

    Lamb bears the weight of truthful confessions.

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is the Clockmaker, during the day they publicly raise their hand and state to the Storyteller about confessing being the Clockmaker to the Lamb, that night, the Lamb learns a thumbs-up (true).

    • Gambit is the Lepakon, during the day they publicly raise their hand and state to the Storyteller about confessing being the Clockmaker to the Lamb, that night, the Lamb learns a thumbs-down (lie).

    • Three players confess to the Lamb today, the Lamb only learns about the first player's confession.

    How to Run

    • Each day, if a player made public statement that included confessing an existing character to the Lamb, mark them with the TRUE or FALSE reminder. During the night, wake the Lamb and signal with a thumbs-up or thumbs-down according to if the first public confession was true or not.

    • The confession should be public, hearable and accessible to everyone, similar to the Slayer's public statement ability.

  11. Prism

    Each day, you may visit the storyteller and learn a statement. Statements you learn are either all true or all false.

    “Someone told me to make my almanac entry more queer...”

    Prism knows what is best.

    Examples

    • Each day, the Storyteller chooses a piece of information to give the Prism... so get creative! Reminder that all information you grant them through the game must be either all true or all false!

    • It is up to the Prism to talk with the Storyteller, not the other way around. This isn’t a public conversation, and the group can’t listen in. It’s private. The Prism can choose to not visit the Storyteller if they wish.

    • A drunk or poisoned Prism might get a piece of false information, even if all their statements should be true, and vice-versa!

    How to Run

    • Once per day, if the Prism requests a private chat with you, take them away from the circle so you cannot be overheard. Whisper a piece of information to the Prism. All information you whisper must be true or false.

  12. Thyme

    Once per game, choose a player & guess their character: if correct, they learn this upon visiting the storyteller.

    Thyme benefits from trust.

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is Thyme, during the night, they are woken up and choose to guess CosmoThere as the Clockmaker, their guess is correct. When CosmoThere visits the Storyteller tomorrow, they will learn about Thyme guessing them.

    • Gold Bars is Thyme, during the night, they are woken up and choose to guess CosmoThere as the Clockmaker, their guess is wrong. Nothing happens.

    • Wolf is the Thyme, during the day, they publicly guess CosmoThere as the Clockmaker, their guess is correct. When CosmoThere visits the Storyteller tomorrow, they will learn about Thyme guessing them.

    How to Run

    • At any point in the game, the Thyme may choose to ask the Storyteller towards guessing another player's character, mark them with the GUESS USED reminder. If their guess is correct, mark the chosen player with the LEARNS reminder. The next time that player manually visits the Storyteller, they learns about being Thyme guessed.

    • The guessed player must manually visit the Storyteller at day, and are not prompted to do so.

    • The guessed player learns even if they visit after Thyme is dead/poisoned, as long Thyme guessed while not dead/poisoned.

  13. Tanya

    You have 2 townsfolk abilities (you know which). If you are mad you are these townsfolk or Tanya, lose this ability.

    “Entertainment, fun times, glory & fame... our bonds are growing closer.....”

    Tanya puts an act among Townsfolk.

    Examples

    • The Tanya receives the Chef and Investigator as their abilities, they learn both how many pairs of evils there are together and that one out of two players is the Baron.

    • The Tanya has the Lamb and Pebbles ability, during the day they nominate players to protect but never reveal that they have the Pebbles ability, instead passing off as a Gold that must nominate each day. That Tanya gets to keep their ability.

    • The Tanya has the Shoonken ability, they ask to be publicly executed and reason towards having a death ability, they lose their ability since Shoonken is the only character that has a death ability.

    How to Run

    • During setup, decide which abilities the Tanya has. Tell them on their first night by pointing at Townsfolk in the character sheet and pretend they are those characters by waking them accordingly to the night order of those characters.

    • "Madness" stands for "You are trying to convince the group of something". So, if the Tanya player is trying to convince the group that they are one of the character abilities they received or Tanya, they must lose their ability.

    • A Tanya generated after the first night still learns characters and gains their abilities, threat them as if it is their first in-game night.

  14. Shoonken

    When you learn that you died, guess up to 3 players characters, you learn how many are correct.

    “I'm a gamblin man.”

    Shoonken gambles.

    How to Run

    • When the Shoonken dies, they may choose to declare privately or publicly up to 3 players and their respective characters. They may take a few minutes to do so, and other players may give advice on who to choose, but it is always the Shoonken’s decision. They immediately learn their information on how many they got correct, either upon visiting the Storyteller or being woken at night.

  15. Pebbles

    Your other nominees are safe from evil. You are poisoned if you nominate an evil player.

    Pebbles wardens town out of evil.

    Examples

    • The Pebbles nominates themselves, nothing happens.

    • The Pebbles nominates a good player, they are now safe from any evil ability.

    • The Pebbles nominates an evil player, they are now poisoned and cannot protect anyone.

    • The Pebbles nominates a good player that registers as evil, they are now poisoned and cannot protect anyone.

    How to Run

    • When Pebbles nominates a good player, mark them with the SAFE FROM EVIL reminder. When an evil ability is used on that player, it has no effect.

    • When Pebbles nominates an evil player, mark them with the POISONED reminder. Their ability lasts no effect until they are cured from poison.

    • The effect is instant, players are protected and Pebbles is poisoned the moment one nominates someone.

  17. Gold

    Each night, receive a goal. If the last goal was not achieved, turn to the alignment that is losing. Even if you are dead.

    Gold is unlucky.

    Examples

    • CosmoThere is the Gold, they receive the goal to "be MAD about being the Clockmaker", they convince town of being the Clockmaker, nothing happens.

    • Wolf is the Gold, they receive the goal to "Nominate a player", they fail to do so. That night, evil is losing and as such, Wolf becomes evil.

    How to Run

    • During the night, wake up Gold and write a remark towards their current goal. If there was a previous goal, announce whether or not they completed it via a thumbs-up or thumbs-down signal. If they failed their previous goal, rotate Gold's character token—right side up for good, upside-down for evil—to match this player’s alignment, then grant them a thumbs-up or a thumbs-down (indicating their new alignment). put the Gold back to sleep.

    Similar to Zenomancer, most goals are more interesting if you make Gold unable to tell them to other players, or reveal themselves in general. Keep in mind that Gold might plan by themselves to change alignment if they don't like their current one, this is intended. It is up to the Storyteller to consider which team is currently losing, and whether changing Gold's alignment fits balance or not.

  18. Dani The Duck

    Each night, choose a player (different to previous nights): you are mad that they're evil tomorrow, or you might be executed.

    “Madness is a form of art.”

    Dani tunnels someone.

    Examples

    Strawberry is the Dani. On the first night, they chose Gambit and made an effort to frame them as evil during the day. On the second night, they chose Wolf, they defended Wolf during nominations and got executed for it.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake up Dani so they can select a player to be MAD about! If you believe they are not following the madness rules, execute them!

    • "Madness" stands for "You are trying to convince the group of something". So, if the Dani player is trying to convince the group that their chosen player is evil, they are following the madness rules. Otherwise (if they are trying to convince people that player is not evil), they are not!

    If you're utilizing Dani on its home script (We are Livetext), you might want to consider claiming their character as breaking madness, not only as the script is balanced around Outsiders not revealing themselves early, but as it makes their "madness" unconvincing in general.

  19. Plasma

    Each night*, you might change ability & alignment to help the losing team & keep this ability.

    “"Through this... let me be a conduit for the wills of god!"”

    Plasma might switch ability and alignment based on what the Storyteller deems the most balanced.

    Examples

    • The Storyteller thinks the good team is winning terribly, and decides to give the evil team an extra teammate. They wake up Plasma, and gives them a thumbs-down, then puts them back to sleep.

    • The Storyteller believes the evil team is winning terribly, and decides to give the good team some help. They wake up Plasma, and points at the Savant on the character sheet. They give them a thumbs-up before putting them to sleep.

    • Plasma is poisoned. Yesterday, they were changed into the evil Devil's Advocate. Today, they wake up to pick a player, but nothing happens. They are good with no ability.

    How to Run

    • When waking up Plasma, show them the YOU ARE info token, then point to a character on the character sheet. Give them a thumbs-up or thumbs-down to indicate their alignment each night, and put them back to sleep.

    • Replace Plasma's character token with their new character token, if their new character token doesn't match their alignment, turn the token upside down. Make sure to mark them with the IS PLASMA reminder.

    • If their new ability needs them to wake up, wake them up when their new character wakes to act. Them put them back to sleep.

    While it's tempting to turn Plasma into Townsfolk/Minions, keep in mind they're still Outsiders meant to harm good, but not actively destructive minions. It might be interesting to turn Plasma into evil Townsfolk.

  20. Mysoury

    Each night, choose 2 players: the 1st is mad the 2nd is good, or 1 might be executed unless it's the final day.

    “Livetext is paved with good intentions.”

    Mysoury spreads mysinformation through truths.

    Examples

    • The Mysoury chooses Gold Bars and CosmoThere. Gold Bars is the Investigator and knows the minion is one of CosmoThere or Wolf. During the day, Gold Bars confirms CosmoThere as good and frames Wolf as evil.

    • The Mysoury chooses CosmoThere and Wolf, during the day, CosmoThere sneaks in Oracle information that frames Wolf as evil, and does not elaborate information that proves otherwise. CosmoThere is executed and the day ends.

    • The Mysoury chooses Gambit and Wolf. During the day, Gambit tells everyone there is a Mysoury in play and that Wolf is definitely a good player, then winks at the Storyteller. Gambit is gruesomely executed and the day ends.

    • The Mysoury chooses two players, then dies. During the day a player breaks madness, nothing happens. Even though that player still believes to be MAD.

    • The Mysoury chooses two players. It is the final day with 3 players alive and one of them breaks madness, nothing happens.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Mysoury. The Mysoury points to one player, then another player. Mark the first player with the MAD reminder and the second player with the 2ND reminder. Put the Mysoury to sleep. Wake the player marked MAD. Show the THIS CHARACTER SELECTED YOU info token then the Harpy token, then point to the player marked 2ND. Put the player marked “Mad” to sleep.

    • At night, Mysoury chooses two players separately.

    • That player is affected by Mysoury's ability until their next choice.

    • "Madness" stands for "You are trying to convince the group of something". So, if the Mysoury chosen player is trying to convince the group that player is good, they are following Mysoury's rules, otherwise? They are not, execute them!

    • The Mysoury can select a dead player, if so, the Storyteller may execute that dead player on a madness break.

    • On the final day, the Storyteller is not allowed to execute in case of a madness break.

  21. Person1101

    Once per game, at night, choose a player (not the Demon): they might register falsely, even if you're dead.

    Person1101 fades town's light.

    Examples

    • The Person 1101 picks the demon without knowing who their demon is, they lose the ability and nothing happens.

    • Loen learns an evil player tonight, a good player chosen by Person1101 registers as evil, Loen learns that good player is evil.

    • There is a Savant in play, there are two evil players and one good player chosen by Person1101. The Savant learns there are three evil players as a true statement, since that good player registered as evil.

    • A good player chosen by Person1101 is nominated by Pebbles, that player registers as an evil character, since Pebbles is poisoned upon nominating evil players, they are now poisoned.

    • There are two Outsiders, one was picked by Person1101 and is 'About to die', Mr Froggit's ability only works if no Outsiders were 'About to die' last day, that Outsider registers as Townsfolk and Mr Froggit's ability still works.

    How to Run

    • Each night, wake the Person1101 accordingly until they decide to choose a player, mark that player with the FALSE reminder and the Person1101 with the NO ABILITY reminder.

    • Each time the chosen player is targeted by an ability that detects or affects them, choose which character and alignment they registers as.

  26. Lepakon

    Each night*, choose a player: they die. Each day, if you are the first to nominate yourself & another player is executed, evil wins.

    “Lepakon Always evil...”

    Lepakon uses unconventional methods.

    Examples

    • Gambit is the Lepakon, they nominate themselves on the first day and are executed, good wins.

    • CosmoThere is the Lepakon, they nominate themselves on the second day, nobody is executed, the game continues.

    • Gold Bars is the Lepakon, they nominate themselves on the third day and someone else is executed, evil wins.

    How to Run

    • Each night except the first, wake the Lepakon. They point at any player. Put the Lepakon to sleep. That player dies - mark them with a DEAD reminder.

    • If Lepakon nominated themselves today, mark them with the SELF NOMINATE reminder, if someone else is executed today, announce the game is over and that evil has won.

  27. Mr Froggit

    Each night*, choose a player: they die. If you die with Outsiders alive & none were 'about to die' last day, a Minion becomes Froggit. [+1 Outsider]

    “Waiter! Waiter! More Outsiders please :D.”

    Mr Froggit convinces others to nominate for them.

    Examples

    • Froggit is executed during the day, there is currently an alive Recluse in play. Since the Recluse is an Outsider, the Storyteller checks to see if the Recluse was 'about to die' the past day. They were not, so a minion becomes Froggit after the original's execution.

    • Froggit kills themselves during the night, there is currently an alive Recluse in play and no alive Outsiders were 'about to die' last day. A minion becomes Froggit.

    • Froggit dies, there is currently an alive Recluse and a Mutant. The Mutant was 'about to die' last day, the game ends since only one alive Outsiders need to be 'about to die'.

    • Froggit dies, there is currently an alive Recluse and a dead Mutant. The Mutant was 'about to die' last day, but is not currently alive. So a minion becomes Froggit.

    • Froggit dies, there are no alive Outsiders the moment they die. The game ends.

    • Froggit dies, a Townsfolk picked by Person1101 misregisters as an Outsider. No alive Outsiders were 'about to die' last day. A minion becomes Froggit.

    • Froggit dies after a dead Outsider that was 'about to die' last day is ressurected. The game ends.

    How to Run

    • While setting up the game, remove one Townsfolk character token and add any Outsider character token.

    • If there are any alive Outsiders on the day that Mr Froggit dies. Check if one of the currently alive ones were 'about to die' last day (that is, has gotten enough votes to be on the block). If this condition has not been met, choose an alive minion tonight and replace their character token with a spare Froggit token. Wake the new Froggit, show them the YOU ARE info token, then show them the Froggit token.

    • Mark Outsiders with the 'about to die' reminder token to keep track of which Outsiders were 'about to die' last day.

  28. Eeb

    If a dead player chooses to lose their vote token, you gain their ability but might lose it at any time.

    Eeb substitutes past players.

    Examples

    • The dead Chef grants their ability to the Eeb, they lose their vote token. That night, Eeb learns a "1".

    • The dead Fortune Teller grants their ability to the Eeb, during the night, the Eeb is allowed to select two players and learns a "yes", the Storyteller then proceeds to tell the Eeb they have no ability.

    How to Run

    • At any point in the game, dead players may choose to lose Their vote tokens and grant their abilities to Eeb, they must publicly ask the Storyteller to do so. When this happens, announce publicly that player lost their vote token and mark them with the HAS ABILITY reminder.

    • Treat Eeb as if they had their according ability by asking them on the right order and prompting choices.

    • The Eeb does not learn which ability they received from that player.

    • The Eeb is allowed to have many abilities at the same time.

    • The Eeb learns their ability loss on private, not publicly, unless the ability involves publicly knowledge, such as the Vizier.

    You are allowed to grant the Eeb as many abilities you find fitting, however i do personally remove Eeb's previous ability and keep them with only two at most to prevent convoluting the game. You might want to discuss such ruling with your group before Storytelling.

  30. Juniper

    Players must start every word with "J", or something bad might happen.

    “Jan je jlay jith jhe jootlegger jnabled?”

    Juniper brings the Jootlegger with them;

    Examples

    • Gold Bars is playing with a Juniper in game, they say "Ji'm Jthe Jstar jdon't jexecute jme.", nothing happens.

    • Gambit says "Hey guys can we exile the Juniper now", they are executed and the day ends.

    • Strawberry, the Juniper, breaks their own ability's rules by saying "I hope Gambit is the demon", Strawberry is now the only player that must respect the Juniper's ability and everyone else can talk as normal.

    How to Run

  32. Wrendle

    Before setup, each player choose two characters. Their ability is based off one of those characters.

    Wrendle turns Livetext into a Lizard's game.

    Examples

    • The Storyteller is playing with Wrendle active. Before the game starts, they privately ask Gold Bars for two characters, Gold Bars answers "Chef or Recluse". Upon drafting the tokens, Gold Bars gets to be an Outsider. The Storyteller decides Gold Bar's ability will be based on the Chef character, and tells them: " Your ability is: If there's no alive pair of evils, a Townsfolk becomes evil".

    How to Run

    • Before each game starts, privately consult each player to figure out which two character options they would like to have their ability based on. Keep in mind each player choice.

    • Once all character are collected, setup a game to figure out each player's alignment and manually assign them the character choice, they keep their original alignment and character type.

    • Privately talk to each player and assign a custom ability that fits both the character they chose and type they have received, example:

    • A player chooses Shoonken, Shoonken's ability is a guessing on death that grants information. Some ideas that fit each character type:

    • Townsfolk: if you died at night, choose a player to guess if they are evil, if you are correct, learn another evil player.

    • Outsider: when you learn that you died, publicly guess two players characters, if you are wrong about any, evil wins.

    • Minion: when you learn that you died, privately guess three players character, correct guesses poison them.

    • Demon: Each night*, choose a character: they die. When you are executed, a minion becomes this.

    Be aware that Lizard game setups take on average 30 minutes to a full hour. Similar to a Lizard game, the pressure on the Storyteller to improvise full homebrew is unmatched.
